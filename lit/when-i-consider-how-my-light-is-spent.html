When I Consider How My Light is Spent" Poem by John Milton

When I consider how my light is spent Explain me

When I consider how my light is spent

Word notes:

  • Consider: contemplate, think about
  • Light: metaphor for talent, ability, or life
  • Spent: used up, exhausted

Paraphrase:

When I think about how I have used up my talents or life

Explanation:

The speaker is reflecting on the use of their abilities or life and questioning whether they have used them wisely.

Historical/cultural context:

The poem was written by John Milton, an English poet, in the 17th century. It reflects on his blindness and his struggle to find purpose in his life.

"Doth God exact day-labour, light denied?"

Word notes:

  • Exact: demand
  • Day-labour: hard work during the day
  • Light denied: a reference to the speaker's blindness
